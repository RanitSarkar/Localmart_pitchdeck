The Widening Gap in Local Commerce

Inflated Costs for Consumers

Aggregator apps inflate prices and add hefty fees, widening the gap between what customers pay and what sellers earn.

Read More
Tourists and locals often pay up to 40% more for goods and food on popular platforms. This premium rarely benefits the local business, instead feeding large corporate platforms.

Invisible Local Artisans

Talented local businesses are digitally overshadowed by large chains, making it impossible for them to reach a wider audience.

Read More
Without a strong digital presence, unique local shops and endemic artisans remain undiscovered by visitors and even residents in their own community, stunting their growth potential.

Eroding Local Economies

Money flows out of the community to non-local corporations, starving small businesses of capital and character.

Read More
This "economic leakage" prevents local entrepreneurs from hiring, expanding, and contributing to the unique cultural fabric of their community, leading to homogenization.

The Vast Potential of Hyper-Local Commerce

The Hyper-Local Opportunity

The global market for local services is immense, but existing digital tools are flawed. We are targeting the growing segment of consumers who prioritize authenticity, sustainability, and community.

  • Total Addressable Market (TAM): The $1.2T global local commerce market shows the scale of the opportunity.
  • Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): The Indian hyper-local market is a rapidly growing $150B segment.
  • Initial Target Market: We will begin by focusing on key tourist and metro hubs in India during our pilot phase.

Our Go-to-Market & Growth Goals

Our community-led strategy focuses on a pilot launch in select cities. We project steady, sustainable adoption by sellers eager for a fair platform and consumers seeking authentic experiences.

Our goal is to onboard 5,000 active sellers and achieve 100,000 active users within the first 18 months post-launch.
